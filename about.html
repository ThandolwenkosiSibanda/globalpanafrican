About Us

Our Foundation and beliefs

Pan-Africanist ideals emerged in the late nineteenth century in response to European colonization and exploitation of the African continent. Pan-Africanist philosophy held that enslavement and colonialism depended on and encouraged negative, unfounded depictions of African people and their culture, values, and contributions to the world. These in turn gave birth to intensified forms of racism which Pan-Africanism sought to eliminate.

The 1945 congress urged the worldwide global Pan African community to unite, to assert their rights, and to reject those seeking to control their destinies. Continuing the tradition, the 8th PAC must encourage Africans to have independent thinking and to gain political power from imperialists. This is a prerequisite for complete social, economic, and political emancipation.

Mission and Vision

Our Vision

Global Pan African Movement believes that African peoples both on the African continent and in the Diaspora share not merely a common history but a common destiny.

Our Mission

  • The mission of the Global Pan African Movement is to organize the Global African Family to build a popular social movement dedicated to the realization of the historical ideals and objectives of Pan Africanism.
  • To mobilize the Global African Family under the banner of Pan Africanism to develop and express our strength through unity, independence, social justice, economic and political democracy, solidarity and freedom.
  • The Global Pan African Movement is committed to the destruction of Imperialism and the elimination of its global network of Euro-centric ideological and cultural hegemony and militarist triumphalism.
  • To recognize the dynamic essence and role of culture and rejects all forms of cultural imperialism especially Euro-centric cultural hegemony. We therefore work for the deconstruction and removal of all institution, ideologies and false historical narratives that negate and distort the humanity, dignity and historical role of Black /African Peoples’ contribution to humanity from antiquity to the present day 21st century.
  • To bring to fruition an all-African Union Government that has the power and moral fortitude to recognize its’ responsibility to protect the intrinsic human liberties and civil rights of all African peoples globally and act accordingly.
